# NPC Relationships

How NPCs relate to each other and to the player.

## Reputation

Each NPC (and each faction) has a reputation score with the player:
- **Allied**: actively helps you.
- **Friendly**: generally positive, willing to do small favors.
- **Neutral**: indifferent.
- **Hostile**: actively opposed.
- **Enemy**: actively working against you.

Reputation changes through:
- Completing quests for them
- Helping them in combat
- Giving gifts
- Insulting them
- Attacking them

## Trust & faction standing

Beyond simple reputation, NPCs track **trust**:
- **Trust 0-3**: stranger.
- **Trust 4-6**: acquaintance.
- **Trust 7-9**: friend.
- **Trust 10+**: close ally.

Trust affects what NPCs will share with you and what they'll do for you.

## NPC-to-NPC relationships

NPCs have relationships with each other:
- Family
- Friends / rivals / enemies
- Romantic partners
- Faction membership
- Mentorship

These relationships drive NPC behavior when the player isn't directly involved (see [[concepts/LivingWorld]]).

## Personality (MBTI / alignment)

Some NPCs are tagged with MBTI types and D&D alignments (e.g., "INTJ, Lawful Neutral"). These tags influence:
- Decision-making patterns
- Speech style
- Reactions to player actions

## Player tips

- **Build relationships early**: a friend made at level 1 is worth more than a friend made at level 10.
- **Watch who NPCs trust**: an NPC who trusts you will share intel about their rivals.
- **Beware of betrayal**: high trust can be exploited by evil NPCs.
