# Spellcasting

Magic-using classes (Cleric, Druid, Bard, Sorcerer, Warlock, Wizard, Paladin, Ranger) cast spells using spell slots.

## Spell slots

You have a limited number of spell slots per spell level, determined by your class and level. A 5th-level Wizard has 4 first-level slots, 3 second-level slots, 2 third-level slots. Higher-level slots can be used to cast lower-level spells (upcast).

Example: a 5th-level Wizard with 2 third-level slots can use them to cast Fireball (3rd level) twice, or to cast Burning Hands (1st level) at 3rd level for +1d6 damage.

## Casting time

- **Action**: most spells.
- **Bonus action**: a few spells (e.g., Healing Word, Shillelagh). If you cast a bonus-action spell, you can only cast a cantrip with your action.
- **Reaction**: a few spells (e.g., Shield, Counterspell).
- **Ritual**: 10 extra minutes, doesn't consume a slot (Wizard, Cleric, Druid, Bard).

## Concentration

Some spells require concentration. You can only concentrate on one spell at a time. If you take damage while concentrating, you must make a CON save (DC 10 or half the damage taken, whichever is higher) or lose the spell.

## Prepared vs known

- **Prepared casters** (Cleric, Druid, Paladin, Wizard): prepare a list of spells from your class list each long rest. Number prepared = spellcasting modifier + half level.
- **Known casters** (Bard, Sorcerer, Warlock): have a fixed list of "known" spells. Less flexibility.

## Cantrips

Level 0 spells. Unlimited casts. Examples: Fire Bolt, Eldritch Blast, Sacred Flame, Mage Hand.

## Player tips

- **Cantrips scale**: at level 5, Fire Bolt does 2d10. At level 11, it does 3d10. Cantrips remain relevant.
- **Concentration is fragile**: avoid stacking concentration spells. If you're concentrating on Bless, don't cast another concentration spell.
- **Upcasting is strong**: a 3rd-level Fireball (8d6) is much better than a 1st-level Magic Missile (3d4+3).
